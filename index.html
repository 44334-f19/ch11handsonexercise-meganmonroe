Copyright Law and the Web

Fact 1: Copying Someone else's web design

You can look for design inspiration on other websites & incorporate some elements into your design. You just can't copy the elements themselves or the code. You have to use your own code to create the element.

Source: Thomas Digital

Fact 2: Image Use

Many web designers face copyright issues when it has to do with images and other media they use in their layouts. It is common to look for images by doing a Google search, but Google itself warns that these images are usually protected by copyright.

It's important to make sure you always have the rights to any image you use in your web layout.

Source: Envatotuts+

Fact 3: Copyright of a Website

Copyright of a website could apply to the visual works of the website, the underlying source code, and/or the client's content that is on that site.

Source: NYU Law

Fact 4: Fair Use

Fair Use is the limited and reasonable use of copyrighted material that does not interfere with an owner's rights. Fair Use is appropriate for public benefit purposes.

Source: Terms Feed

Fact 5: What happens if you steal someone's work

If you are found to be stealing someone's content, images, or any form of work, your site can be shut down by an ISP.

Source: Site Point
